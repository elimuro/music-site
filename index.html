About

Eli Muro is a genre-defying Mexican-Canadian music producer, DJ, and live performer based in Vancouver, Canada. Contributing to the Western Canadian electronic music scene for over a decade, Muro's artistic journey spans from instrumental hip-hop to experimental electronic soundscapes.

As co-founder of the influential Vancouver-based art foundation Chapel Sound, Muro has been instrumental in nurturing the region's musical ecosystem through curation, event production, and music technology education. His musical evolution reflects a deep commitment to sonic exploration and community building.

Muro's studio productions are characterized by intricate rhythmic arrangements and rich atmospheric textures, often incorporating live instrumentalists alongside electronic techniques. This versatility has enabled him to share stages with a diverse range of artists over the years, and perform at Western Canadian festivals including Basscoast, Shambhala, and Newforms Festival.

Beginning his career under the moniker "Headspace," Muro released several jazz-influenced beat tapes, most notably "Salmagundi" (2011), which featured collaborations with jazz instrumentalists. His early entrepreneurial spirit led him to establish Jellyfish Recordings, a net label showcasing instrumental music from Canadian artists, which later merged with other Vancouver crews to form Chapel Sound collective.

Beyond traditional musical formats, Muro has distinguished himself through interactive sound installations, including an 8-channel generative diffusion work at New Forms Festival (2019) and an interactive sound installation at Vancouver Mural Fest (2022). His passionate exploration of hardware instruments and their impact on music creation continues to drive his interest in interactive sonic environments and innovative instrument design.

His deep integration with Vancouver's vibrant cultural scene has positioned him as both artist and advocate, championing the intersection of technology and musical expression. Eli's performances are known for their dynamic energy and seamless blend of carefully crafted compositions with improvisational elements. His work continues to evolve, challenging conventional genre boundaries while remaining accessible to diverse audiences.
